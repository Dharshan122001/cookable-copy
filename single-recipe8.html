Drum Stick Curry

south indian curry is generally multipurpose and is not dedicated to just roti or rice. this is mainly due to the flexibility with the staple food as rice and roti are equally embraced across all the states. hence most of the vegetables are prepared in both sambar and curries variations and the drumstick is no exception to it.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

firstly, in a large kadai heat 2 tbsp oil. splutter 1 tsp mustard, 1 tsp cumin, pinch hing and few curry leaves.
add 1 onion, 1 tsp ginger garlic paste and saute until onions change colour.

step 2

further keeping the flame on low, add ½ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp chilli powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, ½ tsp cumin powder, 1 tsp garam masala and 1 tsp salt.
saute until the spices turn aromatic.

step 3

further, add 2 tomato and saute until tomatoes turn soft and mushy releasing oil.
add in the prepared masala paste and cook well.

step 4

Mix Well And Make Sure All The Ingredients Are Well Combined. Aloo Stuffing Is Ready

step 5

cook until the oil separates from the masala paste.
now add 20 pieces drumstick and mix well.

step 6

add 1 cup water and adjust the consistency. cover and cook for 15 minutes or until the drumstick is cooked well absorbing the flavour.

step 7

finally, add 2 tbsp coriander and mix well. enjoy drumstick curry with roti or rice.

step 8

keep mixing in between to prevent from burning and continue to cook until oil separates.

ingredients

2 tbsp oil

Half tbsp Salt

1 tsp cumin

pinch hing

few curry leaves

1 onion (finely chopped)

1 tsp ginger garlic paste

½ tsp turmeric

1 tsp chilli powder
1 tsp coriander powder

1 tbsp Chilli Powder

1/2 tbsp Cumin Powder

½ tsp cumin powder
1 tsp salt
2 tbsp dry coconut

Curry Masala Paste

2 tomato (chopped)
20 pieces drumstick
coriander
